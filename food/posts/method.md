# Our Method

<p style="text-align: center; font-style: italic; font-size: 1.1rem; margin-top: -0.5rem;">A note on process.</p>

---

Every review published in The Considered Palate follows the same protocol. Consistency of method is what separates criticism from opinion.

## Environment

All tastings are conducted in natural light where possible, seated, with no competing flavours in the immediate vicinity. Strong coffee, toothpaste, and aromatic cooking are avoided for a minimum of thirty minutes prior. The tasting environment should be calm.

## The Three-Pass System

Each product is tasted a minimum of three times before a review is written.

**The Encounter** establishes first impressions only. Texture, aroma, and immediate flavour profile are noted without judgement or conclusion. Nothing is decided here.

**The Interrogation** is conducted at least twenty-four hours later. Whether the first impression holds, or whether it was flattery, is the only question worth answering at this stage. The palate must be consulted again.

**The Verdict** is the critical tasting. Notes are taken in real time. The assessment is formed and does not change.

Where a product proves ambiguous, or where the experience resists easy classification, further passes are conducted at the reviewer's discretion.

## Palate Cleansing

Between products, the palate is cleansed with still water at room temperature. Sparkling water is not used, as the carbonation introduces a textural variable. Tap water is acceptable. Melbourne's is quite good.

## Scoring

The Considered Palate does not assign numerical scores. We use a scaled assessment system evaluating qualities specific to each product. Satisfaction is always measured. Further scales are introduced where the product demands it. A number out of ten would not survive contact with what we do here.

## Procurement

All products are purchased at full retail price. Nothing has been provided by a manufacturer, distributor, or public relations representative. If that changes, it will be said plainly.
